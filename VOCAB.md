# VOCAB (/paper/)

---

## Content Concepts

### admissibility

The property of a claim being valid for inclusion in PAPER.

---

### artifact

A file governed by CANON.

---

### axiom

A normative rule declared in CANON.

---

### CANON

The governance artifact of a scope.

---

### claim

A statement made within PAPER traceable to ledger evidence.

---

### concept

A term defined in VOCAB.

---

### enforcement

The process by which MACHINE evaluates states against CANON.

---

### episode

The atomic evidence unit representing a bounded production session.

---

### epistemic

Relating to knowledge and observation rather than governance.

---

### execution

The realization of state transitions.

---

### governance

The set of axioms that define validity.

---

### inheritance

The relationship by which a scope adopts axioms from a parent scope.

---

### insight

An observation discovered within PAPER that has no governance force.

---

### introspection

The property that VOCAB defines all concepts used by CANON and VOCAB.

---

### ledger

The authoritative record of state transitions.

---

### minimality

The constraint that governance remain as small as possible.

---

### observation

A claim framed descriptively rather than normatively.

---

### PAPER

The epistemic scope for analysis and narrative.

---

### prose

Non-authoritative text within PAPER.

---

### README

The descriptive artifact of a scope.

---

### scope

A directory governed by a triad.

---

### traceability

The property of being linked to ledger evidence.

---

### triad

The set of artifacts (`CANON.md`, `VOCAB.md`, `README.md`) required for a valid scope.

---

### validity

The state of a scope that satisfies all applicable axioms.

---

### VOCAB

The vocabulary artifact of a scope.

---

### workflow

A procedural sequence not permitted in PAPER governance.
